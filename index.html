Research engines

KPM operates a three-engine framework designed to serve distinct economic roles across different time horizons: return generation, capital stabilization, and long-horizon compounding.

KPM Vector

Return engine

A regime-adaptive momentum research program focused on responding to changing market structure, emphasizing exposure control and execution realism.

  • Designed for trending and stable regimes
  • Volatility treated as a structural regime
  • Exit engineering emphasized at the system level

KPM Aegis

Stabilizer

A defensive, probabilistic swing research program built around risk governance, adaptive sizing, and survivability through adverse conditions.

  • Risk-managed first; returns are a consequence
  • Adaptive behavior by market conditions
  • Designed to dampen drawdowns at the system level

KPM Oracle

Long-horizon

A long-horizon capital allocation framework oriented around identifying future economic winners and building patient compounding exposure.

  • Research-driven allocation (not a trading system)
  • Multi-year horizon and uncertainty-aware sizing
  • Complements shorter-horizon systematic work

Capital architecture

KPM is structured as an integrated platform that can evolve over decades—compounding intellectual property, capital discipline, and institutional credibility.
